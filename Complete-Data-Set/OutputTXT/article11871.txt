Makale Başlığı: Citizenship Perception of 1989 Bulgarian Immigrants Living in Turkey
Özet: In this study, it is aimed to conduct a research on the citizenship perceptions of the Turks who came to Turkey from Bulgaria in 1989 as a forced migration. The research was carried out with 18 Turkish immigrants who were born, raised, educated in Bulgaria and immigrated to Turkey in 1989. The ages of these immigrants, who constitute the study group, vary between 49 and 80 and have been residing in Turkey since then. Qualitative research design, phenomenological approach was preferred in the study; The data were collected in 2019 by face-to-face interviews. In the research, the concept of "homeland", which is effective on the "citizenship" perceptions of immigrants who have migrated in 1989, the findings such as "education", "ideas and attitudes", "elements", "dual citizenship and the European Union", which have an effect on the formation of the concept of citizenship, were reached through content analysis. At the end of the study, it was seen that the citizenship perceptions of the immigrants who experienced the 1989 migration vary according to their lives in the two countries and many different situations they encounter.

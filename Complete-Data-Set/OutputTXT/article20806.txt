Makale Başlığı: A case of typhoid fever with deep vein thrombosis
Özet: Typhoid fever is a systemic infection caused by Salmonella typhi. It is a disease caused by food and water sources and is an important public health problem in developing countries. Fever, flu-like symptoms, headache, cough, malaise, nausea and myalgia are the most common symptoms. Venous thrombosis often occurs in the fourth week of the disease and was a common complication before the antibiotic era. In this article, we present a case who presented with high fever, headache, fatigue, diarrhea, had deep vein thrombosis as a result of the tests, Salmonella typhi produced in blood culture and healing with ceftriaxone treatment.

Makale Başlığı: Paid Teaching: A Metaphor Study
Özet: In this study, it was aimed to determine the metaphorical perceptions of school administrators, permanent teachers and paid teachers working in the official preschool, primary, secondary, and high school levels towards the concept of "paid teaching". The method of the study is metaphor analysis which is one of the qualitative research designs. The study group of the research consisted of 146 participants working in Istanbul. As a data collection tool, a semi-structured online form created by the researcher was used. The collected data were analyzed using content analysis. As a result of the analysis of the data, 70 different metaphors for the concept of "paid teaching" were produced by 146 participants. The metaphors produced by the participants were gathered under 7 main categories and subcategories. These categories are: 1) subcategories of “wage injustice” and “injustice in personal rights” under the main category of “injustice”, 2) subcategories of “occupational security”, “social security” and “future anxiety” under the main category of “assurance”, 3) “Labor and Struggle”, 4) “Need/ Necessity”, 5) “Positive reputation” and “negative reputation” subcategories under the “Professional Reputation” main category, 6) “Quality of Education”, 7) “Employment Problem” categories has been. The five metaphors most produced by the participants for the concept of "Paid Teaching" are respectively “Slavery”, “Seasonal Worker”, “Injustice”, “Stepson” and “Injustice”.

Makale Başlığı: ECONOMIC and HUMAN DEVELOPMENT IN THE BLACK SEA REGION
Özet: The aim of this study is to make a comparative analysis that deals with economic and human development in the Black Sea region. The provinces of the region are handled according to the Statistical Regional Units Classification (NUTS). The main macroeconomic variables between the provinces are discussed in the economic analysis. In terms of human development, development levels were measured on the basis of provinces. For this purpose, the human development index (HDI) prepared by the United Nations Development Organization (UNDP) was used. UNDP's index preparation methodology was used in the study. In addition to the average life expectancy, the number of doctors of medicine and the number of hospital beds was also used in the calculation of the health index. According to the results of the study prepared based on the data of 2019, the Black Sea region has a rate of 6% in Turkey's total production, while the West Black Sea region has the lowest unemployment rate. The region is the second region with the least income distribution inequality. The provinces with the highest development in terms of human development are Rize, Artvin, Trabzon and Karabük. The provinces with the lowest values are Gümüşhane, Sinop, Tokat and Bartın. The most successful provinces in development are Trabzon, Giresun and Rize.

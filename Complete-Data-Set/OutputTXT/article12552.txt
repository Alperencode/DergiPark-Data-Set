Makale Başlığı: A Comparative Analysis of Turkey with Previous and Next 5 Countries According to the 2007 Logistics Performance Index (LPE) Ranking
Özet: Performances of countries in logistics activities were first measured by the World Bank with the help of an index called the Logistics Performance Index (LPE), and then it has continued to be measured every two years since 2010. The purpose of this study is to analyze comparatively the immediate five countries above Turkey (29. Greece, 30. China, 31. Thailand, 32. Chile and 33. Israel) and the immediate five countries below Turkey (35. Hungary, 36. Bahrain, 37. Slovenia, 38. Czech Republic and 39. India) in LPE 2007 according to LPE criteria. In the study, analysis was made based on the LPE information measured 6 times by the World Bank between 2007 and 2018. Each country has a score and ranking out of five in logistics performance, which is measured over 6 criteria determined by the World Bank. In the LPE 2007, Turkey ranked 34th with 3.15 points. As a result of the comparative analysis, it has been seen that Turkey is among the best performing countries, ranking 2nd among the 11 countries in 2012 and 2014. In 2018, it has been determined that it is in the 47th place in the general ranking and is one of the countries with the least performance, ranking 10th among the 11 countries.

Makale Başlığı: An Example of the Conflict Between Morality and Law in the Ancient
Times: Crito Dialogue
Özet: Since the day the law emerged, the tension that has arisen between the rules of morality and law has maintained its existence as much as ever. This process is continuing in the presence of changes in individuals' beliefs and thoughts, depending on the changes in the fields such as science, technology, arts. The basic resemblance between law and morality is that they both aim the preservation of the society and therefore the human worth. However, the legal and ethical differences between societies, on the one hand, lead to an increase in political, economic and cultural differences among societies, while maintaining their existence as elements that make the emergence of a universal understanding of law and morality difficult. The relationship between law and morality, which are the normative characteristics of the social life as regulators, has a decisive role in the existence of relations between societies. The purpose of this study is to present the controversy between Socrates and Crito, which Platon put forth in the Crito dialogues. For this purpose, the attitudes and behaviors that can be exhibited in the face of possible application differences between the present legal order itself and practitioners will be discussed

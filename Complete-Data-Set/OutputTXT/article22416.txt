Makale Başlığı: A Palimsest History Example: The Cretan Revolt in Nikos Kazantzakis' Novels (From Forty Years of Respect to Forty Lines or Forty Mules)
Özet: Nikos Kazancakis, a writer of Cretan origin, XX. Listed as one of the best writers of the century. From his birth in 1883 to his death in 1956, he has covered many events. Among these events, there are the Cretan rebellions, which are closely related to both world history and Turkish history. The author, who was an eyewitness to the events in Crete, frequently used this phenomenon in his works. In these works, which also have historical reality, sometimes they are similar from a one-sided point of view. In this study, comparisons were made with the memories of another observer of the same event and the researches on the subject, by quoting exactly the information that the author gave from his father's memories and his own life about this revolt in his works.At the same time, most events and situations that the author did not dwell on but could be determined by historical documents were given systematically. In this study, based on the Palimpsest understanding of history, by adhering to Nikos Kazantzakis' books mentioned in the footnotes and bibliography, 'What is the Cretan Revolts? How did it occur?', 'XIX. What are the reactions of the Muslims and Greeks living in Crete in the 19th century to the events around them?','What is the entourage of the Cretan Revolts in Nikos Kazancakis' books? ','What is the real entourage of the events described in the books?' seeks answers to questions such as by making comparisons through historical documents.
